## About the service

We are trying to make it easy for teachers and school leaders to know if they are eligible for funding and apply for a National Professional Development (NPD) course. 

The service also supports people who are self-funding.

---

## A funded place is not guaranteed 
Each training provider has a certain number of funded places allocated. If a provider runs out of funded places, the user can come back to the service and ‘register with a different provider’, to try to secure their place. 

We predicted that we had enough funded places available for 30% of reception teachers and that we were unlikely to exceed this number of applications for the first cohort, starting in October 2026. 

### How it works 

First, people have to: 

- select their course and choose a training provider 

- register with the Department for Education (DfE) 

- apply with their chosen provider 


DfE checks if they are eligible for funding and then a registration is submitted. DfE passes on this data to the chosen provider via the API (Application Programming Interface). The provider then emails the user with a link to their application form. 

It is only after successfully completing the application that the user is told if a funded place is available or not. 

## The problem we had 

We had to decide how much to tell people about this as it was seen as a ‘low risk’ problem. 

We tried to: 

- warn people right at the start that being eligible for funding did not mean they would get a funded place 

- tell them they might need to try a different provider if this happened
